## What are CBDCs?
A Central Bank Digital Currency (CBDC) is a digital form of a country's fiat currency, issued and regulated by the central bank. Key definitions include digital currency, blockchain, and distributed ledger technology. CBDCs are designed to work in tandem with traditional fiat currencies, offering a digital alternative for everyday transactions. The concept of CBDCs has been around for over a decade, but it wasn't until the COVID-19 pandemic that it gained significant traction. **Statistics show that 60% of central banks are likely to issue a CBDC in the next 5-10 years**, with notable examples including China's Digital Currency Electronic Payment (DCEP), Sweden's e-krona, and the European Central Bank's digital euro.

### Practical Applications and Examples
CBDCs work by utilizing a digital wallet or mobile app, which allows users to store, send, and receive digital currency. Real examples include China's DCEP, which has been tested in several cities, and Sweden's e-krona, which is being piloted with a select group of participants. Step-by-step processes involve downloading the digital wallet, linking it to a bank account, and using it to make transactions. For instance, a user can download the DCEP app, link it to their bank account, and use it to pay for goods and services at participating merchants.
