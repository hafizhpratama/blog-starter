## Core Concepts: Understanding AI Trading Bots
AI trading bots are computer programs that use **artificial intelligence (AI)** and **machine learning (ML)** algorithms to analyze market data, make predictions, and execute trades without human intervention. The key definitions include:
* **Algorithmic trading**: using computer programs to execute trades based on predefined rules.
* **Machine learning**: a subset of AI that enables systems to learn from data and improve their performance over time.
* **Natural language processing (NLP)**: a subset of AI that enables systems to understand and generate human language.
The history of AI trading bots dates back to the 1980s, but significant advancements have been made in recent years, with the development of more sophisticated ML algorithms and the increasing availability of large datasets.

### How AI Trading Bots Work
AI trading bots collect large amounts of market data, including prices, volumes, and other relevant information. They then use ML algorithms to analyze the collected data and identify patterns and trends. Based on the analysis, AI trading bots make predictions about future market movements and execute trades accordingly. The process can be broken down into the following steps:
1. **Data collection**: AI trading bots collect large amounts of market data.
2. **Data analysis**: AI trading bots use ML algorithms to analyze the collected data.
3. **Prediction**: AI trading bots use the analyzed data to make predictions about future market movements.
4. **Execution**: AI trading bots execute trades based on the predictions made.
