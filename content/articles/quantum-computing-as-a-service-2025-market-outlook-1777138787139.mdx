## 3. How QCaaS Works: From Code to Quantum Shot

### 3.1 The End‑to‑End Flow

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Developer writes circuit] --> B{Select SDK}
    B -->|Qiskit| C[Transpile to IBM backend]
    B -->|Cirq| D[Transpile to Google backend]
    B -->|Braket| E[Transpile to AWS backend]
    C --> F[Hybrid Scheduler]
    D --> F
    E --> F
    F --> G[Quantum Runtime (QRT) service]
    G --> H[Shot execution on hardware]
    H --> I[Result retrieval & post‑processing]
    I --> J[Integrate into application]
```

1. **Write** – Use a quantum‑ready SDK to define the algorithm (e.g., VQE for chemistry).
2. **Select** – The SDK queries the provider’s **Hardware Catalog** to pick the optimal qubit type based on circuit depth and error budget.
3. **Transpile** – Circuit is transformed into the native gate set, with optional **error‑mitigation** passes.
4. **Schedule** – The **Hybrid Scheduler** decides whether the job runs on a quantum processor, a GPU simulator, or a hybrid split.
5. **Execute** – The **Quantum Runtime (QRT)** service launches the shot, monitors fidelity, and retries if needed.
6. **Consume** – Results are streamed back, often via a **WebSocket** or **Kafka** topic, for immediate downstream analytics.

### 3.2 Pricing Models Demystified

## 5. Technical Deep‑Dive: What Makes 2025 Quantum Hardware Viable

### 5.1 Gate Fidelity and Error Mitigation

* **Superconducting qubits** now average **99.9 %** two‑qubit gate fidelity, a 0.1 % improvement over 2024 thanks to **3‑D packaging** and **cryogenic CMOS control**.
* **Trapped‑ion systems** reach **99.995 %**, leveraging **laser‑pulse shaping** that reduces stray scattering.

Both platforms employ **zero‑noise extrapolation** and **probabilistic error cancellation** as built‑in services. Providers expose these as **named pipelines** (e.g., `ibm_error_mitigation_v2`) that can be toggled via the SDK.

A QV ≥ 2⁶⁸ is now considered the **“useful” threshold** for most commercial algorithms (VQE, QAOA, quantum‑ML).

### 5.3 Logical Qubits – The First Taste of Error‑Correction

Google’s **Sycamore‑2** demonstrated a **single logical qubit** with a logical error rate of **1 × 10⁻⁶** after surface‑code encoding. While still expensive, the service is offered under a **premium tier** that guarantees **≤ 10⁻⁶** logical error per 10⁶ shots.
