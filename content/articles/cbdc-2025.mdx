### What is a CBDC?
A Central Bank Digital Currency (CBDC) is a digital form of a country's fiat currency, issued and regulated by the central bank. It's a digital representation of cash, designed to complement or replace physical cash. The concept has been around since the 1980s, but recent advancements in technology have accelerated its development. Key definitions include: digital currency, fiat currency, blockchain, and distributed ledger technology. **CBDCs are not the same as cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin**, which are decentralized and not backed by any government. Instead, CBDCs are backed by the government and are designed to provide a secure, efficient, and reliable form of digital currency.

### How CBDCs Work
CBDCs work by using a digital wallet or mobile app to store, send, and receive digital cash. For example, in Sweden's e-krona pilot, users can download an app, link their bank account, and start using digital cash for transactions. The step-by-step process involves:
1. Downloading the app
2. Linking a bank account
3. Receiving digital cash
4. Making transactions
5. Converting digital cash back to traditional currency
This process is designed to be secure, efficient, and reliable, providing users with a convenient and easy-to-use form of digital currency.

Trade-offs include the balance between financial inclusion and financial stability, as well as the trade-off between privacy and security. For example, while CBDCs may provide increased financial inclusion, they may also increase the risk of government control over financial transactions.
