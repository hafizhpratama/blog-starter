Rollups inherit the security of Ethereum’s consensus while processing hundreds of transactions off‑chain. For passive investors, the distinction matters:

- **Optimistic**: Slightly higher risk of delayed finality, but richer ecosystem of mature DeFi protocols.
- **ZK**: Near‑instant finality, sub‑cent gas, and a growing suite of “L2‑only” products that cannot be replicated on L1.

## 4. Risk Management – The “What‑If” Checklist

| Risk | Description | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Smart‑contract bugs** | Code flaws can lead to loss of funds. | Use audited contracts (Audits by OpenZeppelin, ConsenSys Diligence). Prefer **multi‑sig** vaults. |
| **Bridge exploits** | Moving assets from L1 to L2 (or between L2s) can be targeted. | Keep assets on L2 once deposited; only bridge when absolutely necessary. |
| **Token emission decay** | Incentive rewards drop over time, reducing APR. | Track emission schedules; shift to fee‑only strategies before decay. |
| **Regulatory clampdown** | Some jurisdictions may classify certain L2 tokens as securities. | Stay in **non‑governance** utility tokens for passive income; consult local regulations. |
| **Impermanent loss** | LPs can lose value relative to holding assets. | Use stable‑coin pairs, or limit exposure to volatile assets. |
| **Network congestion** | Even L2s can experience spikes (e.g., during a major NFT drop). | Schedule transactions during off‑peak hours; set gas‑price caps. |

**Key Takeaway:** Passive income on L2 is *not* “set‑and‑forget forever.” A quarterly health‑check (balance, APR, gas price) keeps your net yield on track.

## 8. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: Do I need to run a validator node to earn L2 staking rewards?**
*No.* Staking‑as‑a‑service platforms let you earn validator‑level returns without hardware or technical expertise.

**Q2: How safe is moving funds from L1 to L2?**
Bridges have historically been the target of exploits, but the major rollup bridges (Arbitrum Bridge, Optimism Standard Bridge, zkSync Bridge) have **zero‑loss audits** since 2023. Keep only a modest amount on L1; once on L2, most strategies never need to cross back.

**Q3: What about taxes?**
In most jurisdictions, **staking rewards** and **LP fees** are treated as ordinary income at the time of receipt, while capital gains apply when you sell the underlying token. The upcoming EU directive will standardize reporting for L2 tokens.

**Q4: Can I earn passive income with NFTs?**
Yes. Platforms like **Immutable X** (StarkNet) let you stake NFTs for fee rebates, but returns are generally lower and more volatile than pure token yields.

**Q5: Will gas ever go back up?**
Even if Ethereum L1 gas spikes, L2s remain insulated because they batch transactions. The only variable is **L2 congestion**, which is mitigated by the rollup’s scaling capacity (thousands of tx/s).
