## Core Concepts of Crypto Futures
Crypto futures, also known as cryptocurrency futures or crypto derivatives, are financial contracts that obligate the buyer and seller to buy or sell a specific cryptocurrency at a predetermined price on a specific date. The concept of futures contracts originated in traditional finance, but the rise of cryptocurrencies has given birth to a new market for crypto futures. Key definitions include:
* **Long position**: A bet that the price of the cryptocurrency will rise.
* **Short position**: A bet that the price of the cryptocurrency will fall.
* **Leverage**: The ability to control a large position with a small amount of capital.

### How Crypto Futures Work
Crypto futures work by allowing traders to buy or sell a contract that represents a claim on a specific cryptocurrency. For example, a trader may buy a bitcoin futures contract that obligates them to buy 1 bitcoin at $40,000 on a specific date in the future. If the price of bitcoin rises to $50,000, the trader can sell the contract at the higher price, pocketing the difference as profit. Conversely, if the price of bitcoin falls to $30,000, the trader will incur a loss.

## Expert Insights on Crypto Futures
Many people believe that crypto futures are only for speculators, but experts argue that they can also be used for hedging and risk management. Non-obvious knowledge includes:
* **Contango**: A situation where the futures price is higher than the spot price, indicating a bullish market.
* **Backwardation**: A situation where the futures price is lower than the spot price, indicating a bearish market.
* **Basis trading**: A strategy that involves buying a cryptocurrency and selling a futures contract to profit from the difference between the two prices.

### Contango and Backwardation in Crypto Futures
Contango and backwardation are two key concepts in crypto futures that can help traders and investors understand market sentiment. Contango occurs when the futures price is higher than the spot price, indicating a bullish market. This can happen when there is high demand for a particular cryptocurrency, driving up the futures price. Backwardation, on the other hand, occurs when the futures price is lower than the spot price, indicating a bearish market. This can happen when there is low demand for a particular cryptocurrency, driving down the futures price.
