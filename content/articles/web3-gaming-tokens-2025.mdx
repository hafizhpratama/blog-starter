## 1️⃣ Core Concepts: What Exactly Is a Web3 Gaming Token?

| Token Type | Primary Role | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Utility** | Unlocks in‑game features, upgrades, or access to exclusive content | `$AXS` (Axie Infinity) |
| **Governance** | Grants voting rights in DAO‑style decisions | `$SAND` (The Sandbox DAO) |
| **Revenue‑Share / Staking** | Pays a slice of game revenue or protocol fees to holders | `$ILV` (Illuvium) |
| **Play‑Coin** | Directly earned by playing, convertible to fiat/crypto | `$GALA` (Gala Games) |

- **Native vs. Bridged** – Tokens can be minted on a primary chain (Ethereum, Solana) or on a Layer‑2 (Polygon zkEVM, Arbitrum). Bridged assets add liquidity but expose users to **bridge fragility**—a risk that spiked after the 2023 Wormhole exploit.
- **Supply Mechanics** – Fixed caps (e.g., $SAND’s 1 billion) versus inflationary models that mint new tokens to reward activity. The balance between minting and burning is now the litmus test for sustainability.
- **Governance Layers** – Most top‑tier projects have migrated from a single‑owner token model to a DAO where **vote‑escrowed (ve‑) tokens** lock up supply for 6‑12 months, aligning incentives and dampening dump cycles.

&gt; **Key Takeaway:** A Web3 gaming token is not just a digital coin; it is a *multifunctional economic engine* that must simultaneously power gameplay, reward participation, and sustain a viable market value.

## 4️⃣ How Modern Tokens Power Games: A Step‑by‑Step Blueprint

1. **Token Design**
   - *Supply:* Fixed cap + controlled inflation (e.g., 2 % annual).
   - *Utility:* Access to premium quests, NFT upgrades, governance.
   - *Sinks:* Crafting fees, PvP entry costs, NFT mint burns.

2. **Smart‑Contract Deployment**
   - Use **ERC‑20** for fungible tokens, **ERC‑1155** for hybrid assets.
   - Include **anti‑whale caps** (max 0.5 % of total supply per wallet) and **mint‑rate throttles**.

3. **On‑boarding**
   - **Fiat on‑ramp** via MoonPay or Transak.
   - **Wallet‑lite** (Torus, Magic) embedded in the client to avoid “MetaMask fatigue.”

4. **Earn Mechanics**
   - Players earn `$TOKEN` from quests, PvP, and **staking NFTs**.
   - Rewards are minted as **ve‑$TOKEN** (vote‑escrowed). Lock periods: 3 months (seasonal events) to 12 months (core governance).

5. **Revenue Share**
   - 15 % of all in‑game micro‑transactions funnel into a **treasury pool**.
   - Weekly snapshots distribute **$TOKEN dividends** proportional to ve‑$TOKEN holdings.

6. **Exit / Liquidity**
   - Listed on **Uniswap v4**, **dYdX**, and a **CEX bridge** (e.g., Binance) with KYC.
   - Liquidity mining incentives encourage **stable‑coin pairs** (e.g., $TOKEN/USDC) to reduce volatility.

### Real‑World Example: Illuvium’s $ILV

- **Staking:** Players lock $ILV for 6‑12 months, earning a share of **30 % protocol fees**.

## 8️⃣ The Economic Engine: Tokenomics That Actually Work

### 8.1 Burn‑And‑Mint Equilibrium

```
Mint Rate = (Daily Active Users × Avg. Quest Reward) × Inflation Factor
Burn Rate = (Total Transactions × Burn % per Tx) + (Crafting Costs)
If Mint Rate > Burn Rate → Adjust Inflation Factor downward.
```
